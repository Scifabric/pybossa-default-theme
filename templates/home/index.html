Decode Urgent Actions

Help us improve the effectiveness of our campaigns for individuals

Since 1976, Amnesty activists (like you) have helped us respond to more than 25,000 Urgent Action campaigns aimed at defending hundreds of thousands of individuals at risk worldwide. By contributing less than 5 minutes of your time, you can help us to decode an Urgent Action, analyse results and contribute to our ongoing research.

It's simple to take part, and become a Digital Decoder. Here’s how:

1. Read your first Urgent Action

When you launch the project you are presented with a random Urgent Action document to review. Often the first section contains all the information you need.

Check out our Glossary page if you need help with any of the terms used.

2. Answer a few simple questions

Next, answer a few questions to identify details about the individuals involved (e.g. how many, and in some instances, their name and gender) and analyse the results of the Urgent Action.

See our "how to guide" for more details.

3. Contribute to our research

Your answers combine with those from thousands of volunteers from around the world, and contribute to real, front-line research that is helping Amnesty improve the way we campaign for individuals.

About the Project

Why we're looking to Decode Urgent Actions

Our Urgent Action campaigning protects people from torture, helps free men and women who are wrongly - and sometimes secretly - detained, and secures access to medical treatment or legal counsel for prisoners. It even saves lives.

Since 1976 we have issued over 25,000 Urgent Actions and mobilised over 150,000 people to stand up against injustices. This campaigning is based on a very simple idea: when someone is in immediate danger of serious human rights abuse, those responsible receive thousands of letters, faxes and telegrams from all over the world demanding it to stop. This shows the authorities that the world is watching and has the potential to protect that person from further abuse.

We are proud of the effectiveness of these campaigns, and now is the time to analyse the results and finds ways to be more effective in the future.

With your contributions, we want to decode the result of Urgent Actions from the early ‘70s to date. This will help us understand when and where our campaigning is more likely to be effective. Help us improve our this vital part of our campaigning for individuals, families or communities at risk by dedicating just 15 minutes to reviewing these documents.

For us, this is just the start of the journey. We believe that anyone with an internet connection and a drive to make a difference could contribute in real time to core human rights research. We are already planning future projects to document levels of destruction by analysing satellite imagery, to categorize and verify thousands of hours of video footage documenting the war in Syria, or even to alert international decision-makers about possible threats by monitoring hate speech on social media.

Our vision is to inspire this new generation of tech-savvy activists to become human rights monitors and help document human rights violations using technology. We invite you to join us in this, and become an Amnesty Digital Decoder.
